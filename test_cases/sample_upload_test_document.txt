Sample Study Strategy Document
==============================

This short document is for testing the upload feature in the research agent.

Students preparing for a math exam should begin by identifying the core topics that will appear on the test. A focused topic list helps students avoid wasting time on material that is unlikely to be assessed.

Practice problems are the most important part of exam preparation because they reveal whether a student can apply formulas and concepts under realistic conditions. Reading notes alone is usually not enough for mathematics.

Timed practice can help students improve pacing and reduce anxiety during the actual exam. Students should complete at least one practice session under conditions that resemble the real test.

After completing practice problems, students should review every mistake and write down why the error happened. This turns errors into useful feedback instead of repeated habits.

Students should also create a one-page formula sheet while studying, even if the real exam does not allow one. The process of building the sheet helps organize definitions, formulas, and problem-solving patterns.

Healthy routines matter during exam preparation. Sleep, food, and short breaks can improve attention and make long study sessions more effective.

The best study plan combines topic review, active problem solving, timed practice, mistake analysis, and rest.
